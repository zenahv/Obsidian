

$\text{Base hiragana Monographs}:\begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c} &\texttt{a} & \texttt{i} & \texttt{u} & \texttt{e} & \texttt{o}\\ \hline \varnothing & あ & い & う & え & お\\ \texttt{K} & か & き & く & け & こ  \\ \texttt{S} & さ & し & す & せ & そ \\ \texttt{T} & た & ち & つ & て & と  \\ \texttt{N} & な & に & ぬ & ね & の \\ \texttt{H} & は & ひ & ふ & へ & ほ  \\ \texttt{M} & ま &み&む&め&も \\ \texttt{Y} & や &&ゆ &&よ \\ \texttt{R} & ら & り & る & れ & ろ  \\ \texttt{W} & わ&&&&を\end{array}$  $\begin{array}{c|c|c} \texttt{ya}&\texttt{yu}&\texttt{yo}\\ \hline \text{ } \\ きゃ & きゅ & きょ  \\ しゃ & しゅ & しょ  \\  ちゃ & ちゅ & ちょ \\ にゃ & にゅ & にょ  \\ ひゃ & ひゅ & ひょ  \\ みゃ & みゅ & みょ  \\  \\ りゃ & りゅ & りょ \\ &\end{array}\text{Basic Digraphs}$ 

on the $\texttt{W}$ row, only わ and を are used, on the $\texttt{Y}$ row, only や, ゆ and よ are used

<b>し is pronounced "shi",ち is ponouced "chi", つ is pronounced "tsu", ふ is pronounced "fu"</b> 


$\text{Base monograph diacritics} :\begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c|c} &\texttt{a} & \texttt{i} & \texttt{u} & \texttt{e} & \texttt{o}\\ \hline  \texttt{G} & が & ぎ & ぐ & げ & ご  \\ \texttt{Z} & ざ & じ & ず & ぜ & ぞ \\ \texttt{D} & だ & ぢ & づ & で & ど  \\ \texttt{B} & ば & び & ぶ & べ & ぼ  \\ \texttt{P} & ぱ & ぴ & ぷ & ぺ & ぽ\end{array}$ $\begin{array}{c|c|c} \texttt{ya}  & \texttt{yu}  & \texttt{yo}\\ \hline ぎゃ & ぎゅ & ぎょ  \\ じゃ & じゅ & じょ  \\ ぢゃ & ぢゅ & ぢょ \\ びゃ & びゅ & びょ  \\ ぴゃ & ぴゅ & ぴょ\end{array}$ Digraphs
ぢ(ji) & づ(zu) are very rarely used (as well as their digraphs) since じ and ず already exist and sound the same

<b>じ is pronounced "ji"</b> 


With all that, there is :
 - the ん (n) sound, which is a vowel by itself 
 - the っ which indicates consonant lengthening, similar to double consonants in Latin based languages
 - the ー which indicates a longer vowel, similar to adding vowels in Latin based languages

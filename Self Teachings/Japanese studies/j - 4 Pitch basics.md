There's no real need to learn them by heart, bc it will get natural over time

Every japanese word has at most 1 accented mora.

```avatar
image: Random images/Pitch 1.png
description: |
  
  If the accent is on the first one, the tone will gradually lower over time.
```

The accented mora is the one with the highest pitch

```avatar
image: Random images/Pitch 2.png
description: |
  Here, the second mora is accented, so :

  - The pitch rises from the first mora to the accented one

  - The pitch is highest on the accent

  - The pitch lowers after the accent to the last mora of this word.
```

So you pronounce a word differently according to its accent,

>You can differentiate words that use the same kana by their accent

-> You pronounce the first mora with the lowest pitch unless it's the one accentuated

But not every word has an accentuated mora 

<span style="color:lime"><u><span style="color:green">Exemple</span></u></span> 
```avatar
image: Random images/Pitch 3.png
description: |
  This is known as a "flat word"

  The first mora is at slightly lower pitch, then the rest of the word is at a constant "flat" pitch

  All "flat words" are pronounced with this pitch, lower first and flat until the end of the word
```

So the usual rule regarding accented words, the pitch rises until the accented mora is reached, and after, it lowers until the end of the word.
If the accented mora is the first one, it doesnt rise, it starts high
If the accented mora is the last one, it doesnt drop after (depends on the next word)

```avatar

description: |-
  
  If the accented mora is the last one, it doesnt drop after (depends on the next word)
  
  Words like these are called <u>tail high</u> words
```
